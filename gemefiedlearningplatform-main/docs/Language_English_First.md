## Grammar

### Tenses
- **Simple, Continuous, Perfect, Perfect Continuous** in Present/Past/Future.
- Form, usage, common errors.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Present Perfect vs Simple Past  
- I have finished my homework. (recent result)  
- I finished my homework yesterday. (finished at a specific time)
</div>

<div class="exercise">
Convert to the tense indicated in brackets:  
1) She (to go) to school. (Present Continuous)  
2) They (to complete) the task. (Past Perfect)
</div>

### Sentence Construction
- **Phrases vs Clauses**.  
- **Simple, Compound, Complex** sentences.  
- Subject–Verb Agreement.

### Voice
- Active → Passive: object becomes subject.  
- Rule: `Subject + be + past participle (+ by agent)`.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> The chef cooked the meal. → The meal was cooked (by the chef).
</div>

### Speech
- Direct → Indirect: change of pronouns, tense backshift, time/place words.  
- Said to → told/asked, etc.

<div class="exercise">
Change to indirect speech:  
1) He said, "I am happy."  
2) She said, "We will submit it tomorrow."
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Mastering tense forms, voice and speech ensures clarity and correctness.
</div>

## Literature
Provide brief summaries, themes, character sketches, and key lines for:  
- **Short Stories**: plot, setting, conflict, resolution.  
- **Poems**: theme, tone, imagery, figures of speech.  
- **Plays**: acts/scenes outline, characters, dramatic devices.  
- **Essays**: main argument, structure, rhetorical devices.

<div class="example">
<b>Poetry Device Table</b>

| Device | Definition | Example |
|---|---|---|
| Simile | Comparison using like/as | "as brave as a lion" |
| Metaphor | Direct comparison | "Time is a thief" |
| Alliteration | Repetition of initial sound | "wild and whirling words" |
</div>

## Composition

### Letter Writing
- **Formal**: Sender address, date, receiver, subject, salutation, body (3 paras), complimentary close.  
- **Informal**: Conversational tone; no subject line.

### Report Writing
- Headline, byline, date/place, factual body, quotes, conclusion.

### Story Writing
- Setting → Characters → Conflict → Climax → Resolution.  
- Keep consistent tense and voice; use vivid details.

<div class="exercise">

</div>
