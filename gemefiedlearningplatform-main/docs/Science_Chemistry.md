## Acids, Bases, Salts

### Definitions
- **Acids**: Substances that release H⁺ in aqueous solution (Arrhenius). Sour taste, pH < 7.
- **Bases**: Substances that release OH⁻ in aqueous solution. Bitter, soapy, pH > 7.
- **Salts**: Ionic compounds formed by neutralization of acid and base.

### Indicators
- Litmus (red/blue), methyl orange, phenolphthalein; universal indicator pH scale (0–14).

### Reactions
- Neutralization: acid + base → salt + water.  
- Metal + acid → salt + H₂↑.  
- Carbonate + acid → salt + CO₂↑ + H₂O.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> HCl + NaOH → NaCl + H₂O

## Metals & Non-metals

### Physical Properties
- **Metals**: malleable, ductile, lustrous, conduct heat/electricity.  
- **Non-metals**: generally brittle (if solid), poor conductors, dull.

### Chemical Properties
- Metal + oxygen → metal oxide (usually basic).  
- Non-metal + oxygen → non-metal oxide (often acidic).  
- Metal + water/acid/halogen reactions vary by reactivity.

### Reactivity Series
- K > Na > Ca > Mg > Al > Zn > Fe > Pb > H > Cu > Ag > Au

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Zn + 2HCl → ZnCl₂ + H₂↑ (displacement above Hydrogen)
</div>

## Carbon Compounds

### Covalent Bonding
- Carbon forms four covalent bonds (tetravalency). Catenation leads to chains, rings.

### Homologous Series
- Same functional group, successive members differ by –CH₂–; similar properties.

### Important Functional Groups
- Alcohol (–OH), Aldehyde (–CHO), Carboxylic acid (–COOH), Ketone (>C=O), Haloalkane (–X).

### Isomerism
- Same molecular formula, different structures; e.g., C₄H₁₀ (n-butane, isobutane).

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Ethanol (C₂H₅OH) → Ethanoic acid (CH₃COOH) by oxidation.
</div>

## Periodic Classification of Elements

### Modern Periodic Table
- Elements arranged by increasing atomic number; periods (rows), groups (columns).

### Periodic Trends
- Atomic size ↓ across period, ↑ down group.  
- Ionization energy ↑ across, ↓ down.  
- Metallic character ↓ across, ↑ down.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Compare size: Na vs Mg (Na larger). Reason: higher effective nuclear charge in Mg.
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Chemistry studies composition and change; periodicity explains patterns in properties.
