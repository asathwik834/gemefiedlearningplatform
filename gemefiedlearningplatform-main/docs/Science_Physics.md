## Light

### Reflection
- **Laws**: Angle of incidence = angle of reflection; incident ray, reflected ray and normal lie in the same plane.
- **Mirror formula**: \(\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{u}\) (sign convention).
- **Magnification (mirror)**: \(m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{v}{u}\).

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> An object at \(u=-30\,cm\) before a concave mirror of \(f=-15\,cm\). Find \(v\) and magnification.  
Solution: \(\frac{1}{-15} = \frac{1}{v} + \frac{1}{-30}\Rightarrow \frac{1}{v} = -\frac{1}{15} + \frac{1}{30} = -\frac{1}{30}\Rightarrow v=-30\,cm\).  
\(m=\frac{v}{u}=1\) (same size, inverted real).
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Reflection follows simple laws; mirror formula with sign convention predicts image position and size.
</div>

### Refraction
- **Snell's law**: \(n_1 \sin i = n_2 \sin r\).
- **Lens formula**: \(\frac{1}{f} = \frac{1}{v} - \frac{1}{u}\) (for lenses, with sign convention).
- **Magnification (lens)**: \(m = \frac{h_i}{h_o} = \frac{v}{u}\).

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Convex lens of \(f=20\,cm\), object at \(u=-30\,cm\).  
\(\frac{1}{20} = \frac{1}{v} - \left(-\frac{1}{30}\right) \Rightarrow \frac{1}{v} = \frac{1}{20} - \frac{1}{30} = \frac{1}{60}\Rightarrow v=60\,cm.\)
</div>

<div class="exercise">
1) A concave lens of \(f=-15\,cm\) forms an image at \(v=-10\,cm\). Find \(u\).  
2) Define refractive index and critical angle.
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Refraction bends light at interfaces; lens formula predicts image location, magnification gives size.
</div>

### Dispersion
- **Definition**: Splitting of white light into its component colors by a prism due to wavelength-dependent refraction.
- **VIBGYOR order**: Red least deviated; violet most.

## Magnetic Effects of Electric Current
- **Right-hand thumb rule**: Direction of magnetic field around a current-carrying conductor.  
- **Fleming's left-hand rule**: For force direction on a current in magnetic field (motors).  
- **Electromagnet**: Soft iron core with coil; stronger field than a bar magnet.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Draw field lines around a straight conductor; show concentric circles with direction by right-hand rule.
</div>

<div class="exercise">
1) State principles of an electric motor.  
2) Distinguish motor vs generator rules (left vs right hand).
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Current produces magnetic fields enabling motors, relays and electromagnetic devices.
</div>

## Sound
- **Longitudinal waves** in air; speed depends on medium.  
- **Frequency (Hz)**, **wavelength (m)**, **amplitude**.  
- **Speed**: \(v = f\lambda\).  
- **Echo** and reverberation; uses in SONAR.

<div class="exercise">
1) A sound of 440 Hz travels at 330 m/s. Find wavelength.  
2) Define pitch and loudness.
</div>
