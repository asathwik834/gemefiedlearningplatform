## Algebra

### Polynomials
- **Definition**: A polynomial in \(x\) is an expression of the form \(a_n x^n + a_{n-1}x^{n-1}+\dots+a_1 x + a_0\), where \(a_i\) are real numbers and \(n\) is a non-negative integer.
- **Degree**: Highest power of \(x\) with non-zero coefficient.
- **Types**: Linear (degree 1), Quadratic (degree 2), Cubic (degree 3).

#### Factorization Techniques
- **Common factor**
- **Grouping**
- **Algebraic identities**:
  - \((a+b)^2 = a^2 + 2ab + b^2\)
  - \((a-b)^2 = a^2 - 2ab + b^2\)
  - \(a^2 - b^2 = (a-b)(a+b)\)

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Factorize \(x^2 - 9\).
- Using \(a^2-b^2\): \(x^2 - 9 = (x-3)(x+3)\).
</div>

<div class="exercise">
<b>Exercise:</b>
1) Factorize \(4x^2 - 12x\).  
2) Find zeros of \(x^2 - 5x + 6\).
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Polynomials are classified by degree; factorization and identities simplify expressions and help find zeros.
</div>

### Quadratic Equations
- **Standard form**: \(ax^2 + bx + c = 0\), \(a\neq 0\).
- **Discriminant**: \(D=b^2 - 4ac\).
  - If \(D>0\) and perfect square → real, rational, distinct roots.
  - If \(D>0\) and not perfect square → real, irrational, distinct roots.
  - If \(D=0\) → equal roots.
  - If \(D<0\) → no real roots.
- **Roots**: \(x = \frac{-b \pm \sqrt{D}}{2a}\).
- **Sum/Product of roots**: \(\alpha + \beta = -\frac{b}{a}\), \(\alpha\beta = \frac{c}{a}\).

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> Solve \(x^2 - 5x + 6 = 0\).  
- \(D=25-24=1\).  
- Roots: \(\frac{5\pm1}{2} = 3,2\).
</div>

<div class="exercise">
<b>Exercise:</b>
1) Solve: \(2x^2-7x+3=0\).  
2) If roots are 2 and 5, form the quadratic.
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> Discriminant decides nature of roots; formula gives exact values; sum/product help form equations.
</div>

### Arithmetic Progressions (AP)
- **Definition**: Sequence with constant difference \(d\).  
- **nth term**: \(a_n = a + (n-1)d\).  
- **Sum of first n terms**: \(S_n = \frac{n}{2}[2a+(n-1)d]\).

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> For \(a=3\), \(d=4\), find \(a_5\) and \(S_5\).  
- \(a_5=3+(5-1)\cdot4=19\).  
- \(S_5=\frac{5}{2}[2\cdot3+4\cdot4]=\frac{5}{2}\cdot22=55\).
</div>

<div class="exercise">
<b>Exercise:</b>
1) In AP 7,10,13,... find \(a_{15}\).  
2) If \(S_{10}=145\) and \(a=5\), find \(d\).
</div>

<div class="summary">
<b>Summary:</b> APs model constant-difference growth; formulas give terms and sums quickly.
</div>

## Geometry

### Lines and Angles
- **Key facts**: Linear pair sums to 180°. Vertically opposite angles are equal. Corresponding/Alternate interior angles properties with parallel lines.

<div class="example">
<b>Example:</b> If two lines are crossed by a transversal with equal alternate interior angles, lines are parallel.
</div>

<div class="exercise">
1) Find angle x if a linear pair has angles x and (3x-20).
</div>

### Triangles
- **Congruence**: SSS, SAS, ASA, RHS.  
- **Similarity**: AA, SAS, SSS; corresponding sides proportional.
- **Pythagoras**: In right triangle, \(a^2+b^2=c^2\).

### Circles
- **Theorems**: Equal chords subtend equal angles. Angle in semicircle is 90°. Tangent is perpendicular to radius at point of contact.

### Constructions
- Bisect angles/segments, construct triangles, tangents to circle, etc.

## Mensuration

### Areas of 2D Shapes
- Rectangle: \(A=lb\). Triangle: \(A=\frac{1}{2}bh\). Circle: \(A=\pi r^2\).

### Surface Area & Volume
- Cuboid: \(\text{TSA}=2(lb+bh+hl),\ V=lbh\).  
- Cylinder: \(\text{CSA}=2\pi rh,\ \text{TSA}=2\pi r(r+h),\ V=\pi r^2 h\).  
- Cone: \(\text{CSA}=\pi rl,\ V=\frac{1}{3}\pi r^2 h\).  
- Sphere: \(\text{SA}=4\pi r^2,\ V=\frac{4}{3}\pi r^3\).

## Trigonometry

### Ratios & Values
- \(\sin\theta = \frac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Hyp}}\), \(\cos\theta = \frac{\text{Adj}}{\text{Hyp}}\), \(\tan\theta = \frac{\text{Opp}}{\text{Adj}}\).  
- Special angles: 0°, 30°, 45°, 60°, 90°.

### Applications
- Heights and distances with angle of elevation/depression.

## Statistics & Probability

### Data & Representation
- Raw data, frequency table, grouped data, histograms.

### Measures of Central Tendency
- Mean (arithmetic), Median, Mode (for grouped data via formula/graphical ogive).

### Probability (Basic)
- **Theoretical**: \(P(E) = \frac{\text{favourable}}{\text{total}}\), for equally likely outcomes.  
- **Complementary**: \(P(E) + P(\bar{E}) = 1\).
